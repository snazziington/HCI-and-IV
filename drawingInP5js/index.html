Aries

Astronomy

Aries is a constellation in the northern sky between Pisces and Taurus. It doesn't contain very bright stars; its brightest star Hamal has a magnitude of 2.0.


Astrology

Aries is the first sign of the zodiac and governs the period March 21st to April 19th. Its representation as a ram is identified with the Egyptian god Amon and, in Greek mythology, with the ram with the golden fleece.

Taurus

Astronomy

Taurus is a constellation in the northern sky between Aries and Gemini. Its brightest star is Aldebaran, which is the 14th brightest star in the sky with a magnitude of 0.85.


Astrology

Taurus is the second sign of the zodiac and governs the period April 20th to May 20th. Its representation as a bull is related to the Greek myth of Zeus, who assumed the form of a bull to abduct Europa.

 Gemini

Astronomy

Gemini lies in the northern sky between Taurus and Cancer. Its brightest stars are Castor (magnitude of 1.58) and Pollux (magnitude of 1.15; it's the 17th brightest star in the sky).


Astrology

Gemini is the third sign of the zodiac, and governs the period May 21st to June 21st. It's represented by a set of twins in Greek astrology (namely Castor and Pollux), a pair of goats in Egyptian astrology, and a pair of peacocks in Arabian astrology.

 Cancer

Astronomy

Cancer lies in the northern sky between Gemini and Leo and contains the well-known star cluster Praesepe, or the Beehive. Its brightest star, Al Tarf, is quite dim with a magnitude of 3.6.


Astrology

Cancer is the fourth sign of the zodiac, and governs the period June 22nd to July 22nd. Its representation as a crab (or lobster or crayfish) is related to the crab in Greek mythology that pinched Heracles while he was fighting the Lernaean hydra. Crushed by Heracles, the crab was rewarded by Heracles' enemy, Hera, by being placed in the heavens.

 Leo

Astronomy

Leo lies in the northern sky between Cancer and Virgo and its brightest star, Regulus, has a magnitude of 1.35.


Astrology

Leo is the fifth sign of the zodiac and governs the period July 23rd to about August 22nd. Its representation as a lion is usually linked with the Nemean lion slain by Heracles (Hercules) as part of the 12 Labours he was forced to do as penance for killing his wife and children.

 Virgo

Astronomy

Virgo lies in the southern sky between Leo and Libra, and its brightest star Spica is the 15th brightest star in the sky with a magnitude of 1.04.


Astrology

Virgo is the sixth sign of the zodiac and governs the period from August 23rd to September 22nd. It is represented as a young maiden carrying a sheaf of wheat. She is variously identified as a fertility goddess (the Babylonian and Assyrian Ishtar, among others) or the harvest maiden (the Greek Persephone and others).

Libra

Astronomy

Libra lies in the southern sky between Virgo and Scorpius, and its brightest star Zubeneschamali (Arabic for “northern claw,”) has a dim magnitude of 2.6.


Astrology

Libra is the seventh sign of the zodiac and is considered as governing the period from September 22nd to about October 23rd. It is represented by a woman (sometimes identified with Astraea, the Roman goddess of justice), holding a balance scale, or by the balance alone.

 Scorpius

Astronomy

Scorpius lies in the southern sky between Libra and Sagittarius. Its brightest star, Antares, is the 16th brightest star with a magnitude of 1.1.


Astrology

Scorpius (most-commonly known as Scorpio in astrology) is the eighth sign of the zodiac and is considered as governing the period from about October 24th to about November 21st. Its representation as a scorpion is related to the Greek legend of the scorpion that stung Orion to death (this is said to be why Orion sets as Scorpius rises in the sky).

 Sagittarius

Astronomy

Sagittarius lies in the southern sky between Scorpius and Capricornus. Its brightest star is Kaus Australis (from the Arabic for “bow” and the Latin for “southern,” respectively; it is also called Epsilon Sagittarii), with a magnitude of 1.9.


Astrology

Sagittarius is the ninth sign of the zodiac, considered as governing the period from about November 22nd to about December 21st. It is represented either by a centaur shooting a bow and arrow or by an arrow drawn across a bow. The identification of Sagittarius as a mounted archer was made by the Babylonians as early as the 11th century BCE. Fun fact: Sagittarius points towards the center of our galaxy, the Milky Way.

 Capricornus

Astronomy

Capricornus lies in the southern sky between Sagittarius and Aquarius and consists of dim stars; its brightest star, Deneb Algedi (Arabic for “kid's tail”), only has a magnitude of 2.9.


Astrology

In astrology, Capricornus is known as Capricornus and it is the tenth sign in the zodiac, considered as governing the period from about December 22nd to January 19th. It is commonly represented as a goat with a fishtail; in the Greek myth of Pan, in an attempt to avoid the monster Typhon, he jumped into the water as he was changing into an animal's shape. The half above water assumed the shape of a goat while the lower half assumed the shape of a fish.

 Aquarius

Astronomy

Aquarius lies in the southern sky between Capricornus and Pisces. Its brightest star, Sadalmelik (Arabic for “the lucky stars of the king”), has a dim magnitude of 3.0.


Astrology

Aquarius is the eleventh sign of the zodiac and is considered as governing the period from about January 20th to about February 18th. It is represented as a man pouring a stream of water out of a jug, likely because in ancient times the rising of Aquarius coincided in the Middle East with a period of floods and rain.

 Pisces

Astronomy

Pisces lies in the northern sky between Aquarius and Aries and mostly consists of dim stars; the brightest star, Eta Piscium, has a magnitude of 3.6.


Astrology

Pisces is the 12th sign of the zodiac, and is considered as governing the period from about February 19th to about March 20th. Its representation as two fish tied together is usually related to the Greek myth of Aphrodite and Eros, who jumped into a river to escape the monster Typhon and changed into fish, or alternatively, the two fish that carried them to safety.
